## sin

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \sin^2(x) &= \dfrac{1-\cos(2x)}{2} \\
    \therefore \int \sin^2(x) \,\mathrm{d}x &= \int \dfrac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}  \,\mathrm{d}x \\
                                            &= \dfrac{1}{2} \int (1-\cos(2x)) \,\mathrm{d}x \\
                                            &= \dfrac{1}{2} \left( x - \dfrac{\sin(2x)}{2} \right) + C \\
                                            &= \dfrac{x}{2}  - \dfrac{\sin(2x)}{4} + C
\end{aligned}
$$
